---
title: Reliability Strategy
designer:
  title: Cluster Reliability Strategy
  description: |
    Determines how your cluster handles failures and outages. Higher reliability requires more infrastructure, networking complexity, and cost.
  question: |
    What level of reliability does your workload require?
  question_type: radio
  reference:
    title: Reliability in AKS
    url: https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/aks/availability-zones-overview
  answers:
  - key: none
    label: Single Region, No Redundancy
    description: |
      Accept downtime during failures or outages. Recover by redeploying. Suitable for dev/test or non-critical workloads.
    waf_impact:
      reliability: 0
      cost: 0
      operations: 0

  - key: availability_zones
    label: Availability Zones
    description: |
      Distribute nodes across zones within a single region for resilience against datacenter failures. Requires zone-aware storage and load balancing.
    waf_impact:
      reliability: 5
      cost: 2
      operations: 2

  - key: multi_region_active_active
    label: Multi-Region (Active/Active)
    description: |
      Traffic is served from multiple regions simultaneously. Requires global load balancing (e.g. Azure Front Door), data replication, and conflict resolution strategy.
    waf_impact:
      reliability: 10
      cost: 10
      operations: 10

  - key: multi_region_active_passive
    label: Multi-Region (Active/Passive)
    description: |
      Primary region serves traffic, secondary region on standby for failover. Requires replication, failover automation, and defined RTO/RPO targets.
    waf_impact:
      reliability: 8
      cost: 7
      operations: 7
---
